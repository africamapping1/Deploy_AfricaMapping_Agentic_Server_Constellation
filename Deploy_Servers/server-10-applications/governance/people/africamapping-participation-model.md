# AfricaMapping Participation Model

## 1. Overview

AfricaMapping is a geospatial platform built for Africa and supported by a global network of contributors.

It combines:

- local participation
- global collaboration
- structured program development

---

## 2. Core Principle

AfricaMapping prioritizes African residents while remaining open to aligned global contributors.

---

## 3. Contributor Types

### 3.1 Local Contributors (Primary)

- residents
- students
- local professionals

Roles:
- mapping
- validation
- data contribution

---

### 3.2 Technical Contributors

- developers
- GIS engineers
- data scientists

Roles:
- build programs
- improve systems
- support automation

---

### 3.3 Diaspora Contributors

- individuals with African heritage or connection

Roles:
- funding
- mentorship
- expertise contribution

---

### 3.4 Partner Organizations

- NGOs
- universities
- companies

Roles:
- data
- funding
- project collaboration

---

### 3.5 Independent Contributors

- freelancers
- individuals

Roles:
- project-based contributions
- specialized work

---

## 4. Relationship to Programs

Contributors interact with:

- imagery program
- foundational programs (water, land cover, buildings)
- composite programs (addresses, future systems)

---

## 5. Relationship to Teams

Contributors operate within teams:

- Display Engine
- Imagery
- GIS
- Frontend
- Infrastructure
- Governance

---

## 6. Participation Flow

Contributor
    ↓
Team Assignment
    ↓
Program Work
    ↓
Output (maps, data, tools)
    ↓
Platform Growth

---

## 7. Identity

AfricaMapping is not just a tool.

It is a participation identity and a collaborative platform.

---

## 8. Final Principle

People build the platform.
The platform serves people.
