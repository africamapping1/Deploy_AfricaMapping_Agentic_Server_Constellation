# AfricaMapping Communication

## Purpose

This file applies the communication baseline template to the AfricaMapping tenant.

It exists so AfricaMapping can clearly explain itself across different audiences and use cases.

---

## Tenant

- Tenant: africamapping
- Profile: geospatial platform / mapping infrastructure
- Date:
- Status: early to emerging

---

## 1. Elevator Pitch

- One-line explanation:
  - AfricaMapping is a platform for building and sharing mapping and address data across Africa.

- Expanded version:
  - AfricaMapping provides a structured environment for creating, managing, and sharing geospatial data, with a focus on mapping and addressing systems across African regions.

- Why it matters:
  - It supports better planning, logistics, governance, and access to location-based information.

---

## 2. Stakeholder or Investor Pitch

- Problem:
  - Many regions lack standardized, accessible mapping and address systems.

- Solution:
  - AfricaMapping creates a unified platform for mapping, addressing, and geospatial data management.

- Who it serves:
  - governments, GIS professionals, organizations, and public users

- Why valuable:
  - improves data accessibility and enables better decision-making

- Why now:
  - increasing need for digital infrastructure and geospatial data across sectors

- Future potential:
  - scalable platform for mapping services, integrations, and data ecosystems

---

## 3. Positioning Statement

- Space:
  - geospatial platforms and mapping infrastructure

- What makes it different:
  - focus on Africa-specific mapping and address systems
  - integration of mapping, addressing, and platform thinking

- Relevance:
  - addresses a foundational infrastructure gap

- Alternatives:
  - general mapping platforms without regional focus

- Why this approach:
  - local relevance combined with platform structure

---

## 4. Operator Explanation

- Main components:
  - mapping system
  - address system
  - data management layer

- Roles:
  - data managers
  - GIS specialists
  - platform operators

- User interaction:
  - users access maps, data, and services through platform interfaces

- Operations flow:
  - data ingestion → processing → mapping → distribution

- Day-to-day:
  - updating data, managing maps, improving structure

---

## 5. Objection Handling

- Objection 1:
  - “Why not use existing global mapping platforms?”
  - Response:
    - AfricaMapping focuses on regional needs and integrates addressing systems more directly.

- Objection 2:
  - “Is this scalable?”
  - Response:
    - The platform is designed to expand across regions and integrate additional services over time.

- Objection 3:
  - “Who maintains the data?”
  - Response:
    - The model includes local authorities, platform operators, and contributors.
