# Next Strengthening Priorities

## Purpose

This file summarizes the highest-value next strengthening areas identified by the Service Availability Matrix.

It exists so the platform can move forward deliberately instead of expanding in every direction at once.

This is the next-priorities layer of the tenant matrix.

## Why This Exists

The matrix is already strong enough to show that many things are working.

The next question is:

What should be strengthened first?

This file identifies the most important next priorities based on current tenant comparison and matrix learning.

## Priority 1 — Deepen Business-Model Detail

### Why
Both current tenants have business-model anchors, but deeper component files are still missing.

### What to Strengthen
- value proposition
- segments
- channels
- relationships
- revenue or funding logic
- key resources
- key activities
- key partners
- cost structure

### Why This Is High Priority
This is one of the strongest shared gaps across tenants.

## Priority 2 — Deepen Communication Maturity

### Why
Both current tenants have baseline communication, but richer communication maturity is still incomplete.

### What to Strengthen
- positioning
- operator explanation
- objection handling
- future partner or customer explanation where relevant

### Why This Is High Priority
This improves commercial clarity and profile strength.

## Priority 3 — Preserve and Harden the Shared Baseline

### Why
The business minimum baseline is now one of the strongest shared standards in the platform.

### What to Strengthen
- keep it visible
- keep future tenants aligned to it
- prevent drift in new tenant creation

### Why This Is High Priority
This is the foundation of future onboarding quality.

## Priority 4 — Clarify Profile-Specific Expectations

### Why
The platform now has usable role-surface and stakeholder/service-module patterns.

### What to Strengthen
- clearer expected bundle contents
- clearer profile fit interpretation
- clearer profile-aware parity logic

### Why This Is High Priority
This prevents false parity and weak onboarding.

## Priority 5 — Prepare Matrix-to-Provisioning Connection

### Why
The matrix is strong conceptually but still early operationally.

### What to Strengthen
- provisioning hooks
- onboarding sequence
- registration profile use
- review habit

### Why This Is High Priority
This is the bridge from theory to operational control.

## Lower Priority for Now

These should continue, but not outrun the stronger priorities above:

- integration-oriented bundle standardization
- advanced hybrid profile standardization
- strict blocking logic
- heavy automation too early

## Final Interpretation

The matrix already knows a lot.

The best next move is not to add more theory first.

The best next move is to strengthen the areas that will most improve:
- tenant clarity
- onboarding quality
- parity quality
- future operational control
