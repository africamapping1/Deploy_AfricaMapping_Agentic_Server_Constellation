# Business Framework Control Target

## Purpose

This layer defines the business framework as a formal control target of the Service Availability Matrix.

It exists so the matrix can treat the business framework as a real evaluable surface rather than as background documentation only.

This is the business-framework control-target layer of the Service Availability Matrix.

## Why This Exists

The business framework now defines:

- minimum business meaning
- business completeness
- business readiness
- comparison basis
- profile-aware expectations
- summary discipline
- promotion-candidate thinking
- review rhythm
- intended SAM socketing

That means it is no longer only explanatory.

It is now structured enough to become a matrix control target.

## What This Means

When the business framework becomes a control target, the matrix should be able to ask questions such as:

- is minimum business definition present
- is the business complete enough to compare
- is the business ready for stronger governance use
- are summaries disciplined
- are profile expectations being respected
- are repeated strengths visible as promotion candidates

## Main Interpretation

The business framework is one of the first forward surfaces that SAM is meant to read.

It sits between:

- reusable business truth
- tenant application expression
- later backend review and control

## Important Note

This does not mean the business framework is fully automated.

It means the platform now recognizes it as something that can be deliberately evaluated.

## Final Principle

A matrix becomes more useful when it can read the structures it was built to govern.

This layer exists so business-framework evaluation becomes explicit.
