# Manual Cost Bands

## Purpose

This layer describes rough cost bands for the kind of manual work represented by the Service Availability Matrix.

It exists so the platform can understand the economic weight of this kind of structured governance and documentation work.

This is the cost-band layer of the Service Availability Matrix.

## Why This Exists

The matrix is a real work product.

If produced manually, it would draw on labor from roles such as:

- technical writer
- business analyst
- systems analyst
- product or operations roles

That labor has real cost.

## Rough Costing Logic

The total cost depends on:

- who is doing the work
- whether the person already understands the architecture
- whether the work is local drafting or cross-team coordination
- whether the work is one-time or kept actively maintained

## Simplified Manual Cost View

### Lower Manual Band
Typical of:
- strong generalist
- lighter documentation-centered work
- less coordination overhead

### Middle Manual Band
Typical of:
- analyst-heavy work
- stronger cross-tenant comparison work
- more maturity and governance judgment

### Higher Manual Band
Typical of:
- product/ops or senior architecture oversight
- multi-role coordination
- high-cost local labor markets
- repeated review and revision cycles

## Important Note

This layer is intentionally qualitative.

Specific compensation and wage numbers change over time and geography.

Those figures are better treated as external market data rather than permanent matrix truth.

## Final Principle

The matrix is not free work hidden inside structure.

This layer exists so the platform remains aware that this kind of coherence usually costs real human time and money.
