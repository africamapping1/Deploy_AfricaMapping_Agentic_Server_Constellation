# Business Framework Promotion Signal

## Purpose

This layer defines how the Service Availability Matrix should detect promotion signals inside the business framework.

It exists so the platform can recognize repeated and stable business-framework strengths that may deserve broader reuse or standardization.

This is the promotion-signal layer for business-framework evaluation.

## Why This Exists

Some framework patterns begin as local thinking.

Over time, repeated use may show that they deserve a more formal role.

The matrix should notice that.

## Promotion Signal Questions

The matrix should consider questions such as:

### 1. Is the pattern repeated
Does it appear in more than one business surface or context?

### 2. Is the pattern stable
Does its meaning hold across uses?

### 3. Is the pattern useful
Does it improve clarity, review, onboarding, or comparison?

### 4. Is the pattern specific enough to promote
Or is it still too vague or general?

### 5. Is the pattern reusable
Could it help future tenants or framework work?

## Promotion Signal Outcomes

### No Clear Signal
The pattern is still too local, weak, or immature.

### Emerging Signal
The pattern may be promising, but still needs more repetition or stabilization.

### Strong Signal
The pattern is now a realistic candidate for stronger standardization or reuse.

## Important Note

A strong promotion signal is not the same as automatic promotion.

It is evidence for further governance judgment.

## Final Principle

A framework becomes smarter when repetition can be recognized.

This layer exists so the matrix can notice that repetition.
