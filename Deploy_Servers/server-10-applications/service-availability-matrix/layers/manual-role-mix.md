# Manual Role Mix

## Purpose

This layer defines the types of human roles that would usually contribute to work like the Service Availability Matrix in a pre-AI or lightly assisted environment.

It exists so the platform can understand the human job mix behind this kind of structured governance work.

This is the role-mix layer of the Service Availability Matrix.

## Why This Exists

The matrix combines several kinds of work:

- architecture interpretation
- documentation
- operations framing
- taxonomy design
- comparison logic
- onboarding logic
- governance reasoning

These are not usually all owned by the same traditional role.

## Typical Roles

### 1. Business Analyst
Likely owns:
- requirements logic
- comparison logic
- business baselines
- process mapping

### 2. Systems Analyst
Likely owns:
- structure interpretation
- system classification
- operating model mapping
- readiness and dependency thinking

### 3. Technical Writer
Likely owns:
- file organization
- readable definitions
- summary clarity
- naming consistency
- maintenance of documentation quality

### 4. Product / Operations Lead
Likely owns:
- practical usefulness
- onboarding implications
- standardization judgment
- rollout thinking

### 5. Architect / Senior Reviewer
Likely owns:
- boundary correctness
- alignment with broader platform structure
- validation of what should remain local versus shared

## Small-Team Reality

In smaller environments, one person may perform several of these roles at once.

That increases speed in some cases, but also increases cognitive load.

## Final Principle

The matrix sits at the intersection of multiple real job functions.

This layer exists so the platform understands that kind of work as multi-role architecture work, not just “documentation.”
