# Business Framework Comparison Readiness

## Purpose

This layer defines how the Service Availability Matrix should judge whether the business framework is ready to support fair comparison across business surfaces and tenants.

It exists so the platform can avoid premature or misleading comparison.

This is the comparison-readiness layer for business-framework evaluation.

## Why This Exists

Comparison is one of the most powerful uses of the matrix.

It is also one of the easiest places to create distortion if the framework is not sufficiently prepared.

## Comparison Readiness Questions

The matrix should consider questions such as:

### 1. Are comparison terms defined clearly
Does the framework define the basis on which business surfaces are being compared?

### 2. Are comparison boundaries stated clearly
Does the framework distinguish between:
- framework and tenant
- role-surface and stakeholder-service-module
- conceptual and operational layers

### 3. Are maturity differences being respected
Does the framework avoid forcing strong comparison across radically different levels of completeness or readiness?

### 4. Are profile differences being interpreted fairly
Does the framework preserve comparability without denying genuine profile difference?

### 5. Are summary surfaces trustworthy enough to assist comparison
Can summary layers support comparison without introducing major distortion?

## Comparison Readiness Outcomes

### Weak
The framework is not yet ready to support reliable comparison.

### Partial
Some comparison is possible, but caution is needed.

### Strong
The framework can support disciplined comparison with reasonable trust.

## Important Note

Strong comparison readiness does not mean all businesses become the same.

It means their similarities and differences can be seen more truthfully.

## Final Principle

Comparison is strongest when the framework prepares it honestly.

This layer exists so the matrix can judge that readiness.
