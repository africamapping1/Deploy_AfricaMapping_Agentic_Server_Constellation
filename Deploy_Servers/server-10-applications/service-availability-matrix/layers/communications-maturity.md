# Communications Maturity

## Purpose

This layer defines how the platform should evaluate the communication readiness of each tenant business.

It exists so the system can distinguish between a tenant that has a business structure and a tenant that can actually explain, position, and represent itself clearly.

This is the communications layer of the Service Availability Matrix.

## Why This Exists

A business can have:

- a README
- a business model
- a canvas
- a strategy layer

and still be weak in communication.

That means the tenant may exist structurally while remaining poor at:

- introducing itself
- explaining value
- tailoring messaging by audience
- responding to skepticism
- sounding credible in stakeholder, customer, or investor settings

This layer checks whether the tenant can speak for itself in a usable way.

## Core Principle

Communication maturity is not only about having words.

It is about having the right communication structures for the business and its likely audiences.

A business is more mature when it can explain itself clearly across relevant contexts.

## Minimum Communication Baseline

At this time, each tenant should ideally have at least:

### 1. Communications README
The tenant should define its communication layer.

### 2. Short-Form Pitch
The tenant should have at least one short-form pitch such as an elevator pitch.

This is the minimum “introduce the business clearly” capability.

### 3. Longer Audience Pitch
The tenant should have at least one longer audience-facing communication such as:

- investor pitch
- stakeholder pitch
- partner explanation
- customer story

The exact type may differ by tenant.

The important thing is that the tenant can explain itself beyond the shortest version.

## Expanded Communication Maturity

A more mature communication layer may include:

- elevator pitch
- stakeholder pitch
- investor pitch
- customer story
- partner explanation
- operator explanation
- positioning
- objection handling
- internal explanation
- narrative framing

Not every tenant needs every item immediately.

But the matrix should track what exists, what is missing, and what may be needed.

## Maturity Logic

Useful maturity states for communication items may include:

- missing
- placeholder
- scaffolded
- documented
- audience-ready
- reusable

This helps the platform distinguish between a file that merely exists and a communication asset that is genuinely useful.

## Audience Relevance Rule

Communication maturity should not be judged only by quantity.

It should be judged by relevance.

For example:

- a stakeholder-heavy tenant may need stakeholder and partner explanation more than investor pitch
- a product-heavy tenant may need customer story and positioning more than institutional narrative
- a real estate intelligence tenant may need investor and operator explanation earlier than community-facing narrative

The question is not whether every tenant has identical communication artifacts.

The question is whether each tenant has communication coverage that fits its business reality.

## Comparison Rule

This layer should support comparisons such as:

- does each tenant have a short-form pitch
- does each tenant have at least one deeper audience-facing communication
- where are the gaps
- where are the communication patterns converging into reusable standards

## Reproducibility Logic

If the same communication patterns appear across multiple tenants, they may become part of the standard tenant communication baseline.

Examples may include:

- elevator pitch
- communications README
- one deeper audience-facing pitch
- future positioning file

This allows repeated tenant practice to become platform learning.

## Governance Value

A tenant with stronger communication maturity is easier to:

- explain
- onboard
- present
- support
- compare
- scale
- represent through the business-agent

Communication maturity is therefore both a business and governance concern.

## Final Principle

A tenant business is more mature when it can explain itself clearly, credibly, and in audience-appropriate ways.

This layer exists to make that maturity visible.
