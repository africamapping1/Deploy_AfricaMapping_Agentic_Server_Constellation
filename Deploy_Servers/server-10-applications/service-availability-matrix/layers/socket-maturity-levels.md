# Socket Maturity Levels

## Purpose

This layer defines the maturity levels by which a Service Availability Matrix subsection may become connected to the backend side of the platform.

It exists so socketing can progress in stages rather than as an all-or-nothing event.

This is the socket-maturity layer of the Service Availability Matrix.

## Why This Exists

A matrix subsection may become useful long before it is fully automated.

The platform needs a way to recognize partial operational connection.

## Socket Maturity Levels

### Level 0 — Unsocketed
The subsection exists as documentation only.

It has no deliberate operational connection yet.

### Level 1 — Human Review Socket
The subsection is used during human review, checklist work, or structured assessment.

It influences thought and review behavior.

### Level 2 — Agent-Assisted Socket
The subsection is used by agents for drafting, comparison, reminders, or review support.

It influences assisted operating behavior.

### Level 3 — Monitoring Socket
The subsection produces or informs signals, summaries, or visibility inside platform health or status reporting.

It becomes observable.

### Level 4 — Governance Socket
The subsection informs approval, promotion, exception, or escalation decisions.

It becomes part of platform judgment.

### Level 5 — Provisioning Socket
The subsection affects onboarding, scaffolding, or baseline creation behavior.

It begins shaping system generation and setup.

### Level 6 — Enforcement Socket
The subsection can trigger warnings, gates, low-risk corrections, or restricted automation behavior.

It becomes active control.

## Important Note

Different subsections may mature at different speeds.

A matrix is healthy when socket maturity is visible and honest, not artificially uniform.

## Final Principle

Socketing is a staged progression.

This layer exists so the platform can grow operational connection gradually and deliberately.
