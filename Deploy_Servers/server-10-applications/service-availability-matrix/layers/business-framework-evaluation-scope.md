# Business Framework Evaluation Scope

## Purpose

This layer defines what parts of the business framework should fall inside Service Availability Matrix evaluation scope.

It exists so the platform evaluates the right surfaces and avoids forcing the matrix into areas that are still too exploratory or too indirect.

This is the evaluation-scope layer for the business framework inside the Service Availability Matrix.

## Why This Exists

The business framework contains different kinds of material.

Some parts are already structured enough for evaluation.

Other parts may still be exploratory, illustrative, or too early.

The matrix should know what it is trying to evaluate.

## In-Scope Areas

The following kinds of business-framework surfaces are strongly in scope:

### 1. Minimum Definition Surfaces
These define whether a business exists meaningfully.

### 2. Completeness and Readiness Surfaces
These define whether a business can be carried forward in a disciplined way.

### 3. Comparison Surfaces
These define whether fair business comparison is possible.

### 4. Profile Expectation Surfaces
These define how strength should be interpreted according to business type.

### 5. Summary Discipline Surfaces
These define whether compressed views remain trustworthy.

### 6. Promotion-Candidate Surfaces
These define whether repeated strengths are becoming reusable standards.

## Lightly In-Scope Areas

These may be evaluated more gently at first:

- canvases
- perspectives
- communications
- framework-level narrative surfaces

These are useful, but may need more interpretive caution.

## Out-of-Scope for Now

The matrix should avoid strong evaluation of:

- rough brainstorming notes
- partial drafts with no stated role
- unanchored examples
- materials not yet intended as stable framework truth

## Final Principle

A strong matrix begins by evaluating what is stable enough to be judged.

This layer exists so business-framework evaluation stays disciplined.
