# Business Perspectives

## Purpose

This directory contains reusable business perspectives for the application plane.

A perspective is a way of viewing the same business through a specific lens.

Different perspectives help the system understand that a business is not seen the same way by every audience, role, or decision context.

These perspectives are reusable.

They are not tenant-specific by themselves.

They are the lenses through which tenant businesses can be understood, communicated, and governed.

## Why This Exists

A business is not only one thing.

The same business can be viewed as:

- an operating system
- a customer-serving model
- a stakeholder relationship structure
- a strategic growth vehicle
- a communication problem
- a coordination mechanism
- a governed entity
- a portfolio of capabilities

Without structured perspectives, the system risks speaking about the business in only one narrow way.

This directory exists to make business understanding multi-dimensional.

## Core Principle

A perspective does not change the business.

It changes the lens through which the business is interpreted.

The business remains the same.

The view changes.

## What Belongs Here

Reusable business perspectives may include:

- customer perspective
- stakeholder perspective
- operator perspective
- strategic perspective
- financial perspective
- service perspective
- governance perspective
- growth perspective
- portfolio perspective
- partner perspective

The exact set may grow over time.

## Relationship to Canvases

A canvas helps describe the whole business.

A perspective helps describe the business from one angle.

Example:

- a canvas may show the full business picture
- a stakeholder perspective may emphasize visibility, trust, and coordination
- a customer perspective may emphasize clarity, relevance, and delivered value
- a strategic perspective may emphasize scale, direction, and long-term leverage

The canvas is the whole picture.

The perspective is the chosen angle.

## Relationship to Communications

Different communications modes should speak from different perspectives.

Examples:

- an investor pitch may speak from a strategic and financial perspective
- a customer explanation may speak from a customer value perspective
- an employee explanation may speak from an operator perspective
- a partner explanation may speak from a partner and integration perspective

This means perspectives help make communication more accurate and audience-aware.

## Relationship to the Business Model

The business model provides structural truth.

Perspectives do not replace that structure.

They help interpret that structure differently depending on the question being asked.

The model answers:

- what is structurally true about the business

A perspective answers:

- what matters most from this angle

## Relationship to Tenants

Each tenant should eventually have tenant-specific perspective documents under its business space.

Examples:

- `apps/africamapping/business/canvas/`
- `apps/realestate-ai/business/canvas/`
- future tenant-specific perspective notes tied to stakeholder, customer, operator, or strategic views

The reusable perspective logic belongs here.

The tenant-specific use of those perspectives belongs in the tenant business directories.

## Relationship to the Business-Agent

The business-agent should be able to shift perspective without losing business truth.

That means the agent should understand how to talk about the same business differently depending on whether it is acting in:

- customer mode
- investor mode
- operator mode
- stakeholder mode
- partner mode
- strategic advisory mode

These perspective structures help make that possible.

## Quality Rule

A useful perspective should be:

- coherent
- role-aware
- relevant
- non-contradictory
- aligned with the business model
- aligned with the canvas
- useful for communication and decision-making

A weak perspective is one that is vague, redundant, or disconnected from real business meaning.

## Final Principle

A mature business is not understood through one static description.

It is understood through multiple valid perspectives that remain grounded in the same underlying business truth.
