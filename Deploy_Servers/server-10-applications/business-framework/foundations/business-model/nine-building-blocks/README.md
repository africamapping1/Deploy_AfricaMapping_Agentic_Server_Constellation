# Nine Building Blocks

## Purpose

The nine building blocks define the structural model of a business.

They provide a reusable framework for describing how a business creates value, delivers value, sustains operations, and captures returns.

This structure is generic.

It is not one tenant’s business by itself.

It is the reusable model that tenant businesses can fill with real business meaning.

## Why This Exists

A business should not be represented only through scattered notes, intuition, or ad hoc explanations.

The system needs a structured way to understand:

- who the business serves
- what value it provides
- how it reaches customers
- how it sustains relationships
- how it makes money
- what resources and activities it depends on
- what partnerships matter
- what cost structure supports it

The nine building blocks provide that structure.

## Core Principle

The business model is the structural truth of the business.

It defines the architecture of value creation.

It does not replace strategy, communications, or operations.

It provides the core frame on top of which those can be built.

## The Nine Blocks

### 1. Customer Segments

Defines the groups of people, organizations, or actors the business serves.

Questions include:

- who is the business for
- who benefits most
- who pays
- who influences adoption
- who must be prioritized first

### 2. Value Propositions

Defines what the business offers and why it matters.

Questions include:

- what problem is being solved
- what need is being addressed
- what improvement is being delivered
- why this offering matters to the chosen customer segments

### 3. Channels

Defines how the business reaches and delivers value to its audiences.

Questions include:

- how the business is discovered
- how customers engage
- how delivery happens
- how communication moves across touchpoints

### 4. Customer Relationships

Defines how the business builds, maintains, and evolves relationships with the people it serves.

Questions include:

- what kind of trust is required
- what support model exists
- how retention is sustained
- what relationship style fits the business model

### 5. Revenue Streams

Defines how the business captures value economically.

Questions include:

- how money is earned
- what customers pay for
- what payment logic exists
- how recurring or one-time value is captured

### 6. Key Resources

Defines the assets required for the business to operate effectively.

These may include:

- people
- data
- software
- infrastructure
- brand
- intellectual property
- licenses
- networks
- capital

### 7. Key Activities

Defines the core activities the business must perform to create and deliver value.

These may include:

- product development
- service delivery
- client operations
- sales
- analysis
- logistics
- support
- compliance
- improvement

### 8. Key Partners

Defines the external parties, systems, or institutions that meaningfully support the business.

These may include:

- technology partners
- suppliers
- agencies
- distribution allies
- data providers
- channel partners
- financial partners
- strategic collaborators

### 9. Cost Structure

Defines the major cost commitments required for the business to function.

Questions include:

- what must be paid for
- what drives operating expense
- what scales with growth
- what fixed cost must be sustained
- what cost pressures threaten resilience

## What the Model Does

This model helps the system:

- compare businesses
- identify gaps
- understand dependencies
- structure strategy work
- ground communications in real business architecture
- support investor, customer, and internal narratives with real business logic

## What the Model Does Not Do

The nine building blocks do not fully replace:

- strategy
- communications
- operations design
- financial modeling
- governance
- execution planning

They are foundational, but not exhaustive.

## Relationship to Communications

The business model defines what is structurally true.

The communications framework defines how that truth is expressed.

Example:

- the value proposition belongs in the business model
- the investor pitch uses that value proposition in a persuasive context
- the customer message translates that value proposition into practical relevance

## Relationship to Tenants

Each tenant should fill in its own version of the nine building blocks under its business directory.

Examples:

- `apps/africamapping/business/business-model/`
- `apps/realestate-ai/business/business-model/`

Those tenant-specific files should express the real content of the business.

## Relationship to the Business-Agent

The business-agent should not invent the business model from nowhere.

It should speak from this structure.

That means the nine building blocks give the system durable business truth, and the business-agent uses that truth in adaptive communication and decision support.

## Outcome

The nine building blocks allow the system to understand a business as a structured economic and operational entity, not just a collection of activities or messages.
