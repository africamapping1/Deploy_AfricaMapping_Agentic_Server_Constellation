# Business Comparison Basis

## Purpose

This file defines the basis on which business surfaces should be compared inside the business framework.

It exists so comparison can remain fair, useful, and structured.

This is the comparison-basis layer of the business-framework governance branch.

## Why This Exists

The platform should not compare businesses randomly.

Comparison becomes misleading when it ignores:

- different profiles
- different maturity levels
- different operating surfaces
- different scopes

The platform needs a clear basis for comparison.

## Main Comparison Basis

Business surfaces should be compared using the following types of questions:

### 1. Identity Basis
Are these businesses the same kind of thing?

Examples:
- same type of tenant
- same type of service
- same type of business role
- same general scope

### 2. Purpose Basis
Do they exist to solve similar kinds of problems or serve similar classes of need?

### 3. Structural Basis
Are they described through comparable framework elements?

Examples:
- both have building-block coverage
- both have communication framing
- both have clear stakeholder definition

### 4. Maturity Basis
Are they at comparable levels of business completeness and readiness?

### 5. Surface Basis
Are they being compared at the same layer?

Examples:
- framework to framework
- tenant to tenant
- communications to communications
- not mixed arbitrarily

## What Comparison Should Avoid

The platform should avoid forced comparison between:

- conceptually different profiles
- businesses at radically different maturity levels
- reusable framework pieces and tenant-specific execution surfaces
- exploratory notes and stable business definitions

## Important Note

Comparison is strongest when it is used to clarify differences, not erase them.

## Final Principle

A useful comparison begins by comparing like with like.

This file exists so business comparison supports truth rather than confusion.
