# Business Promotion Candidates

## Purpose

This file explains what kinds of business framework patterns may become candidates for promotion into stronger shared standards.

It exists so repeated business strengths can be recognized and carried forward.

This is the promotion-candidates layer of the business-framework governance branch.

## Why This Exists

Some business patterns begin as local explanation.

Later, they may prove stable enough to become shared structure, default guidance, or framework baseline.

The platform should notice that.

## Promotion Candidate Signals

A business pattern may be a promotion candidate when it is:

### 1. Repeated
It appears across multiple business surfaces or tenants.

### 2. Stable
Its meaning does not shift dramatically across uses.

### 3. Useful
It improves clarity, comparison, review, or onboarding.

### 4. Distinct
It expresses something specific enough to deserve its own place.

### 5. Reusable
It can help future business surfaces without being too local or narrow.

## Examples of Possible Promotion Areas

Examples may include:

- recurring business component patterns
- recurring communication structures
- recurring stakeholder framing
- recurring onboarding expectations
- recurring summary formats

## Important Note

Promotion should not be automatic.

A pattern may be repeated but still too immature, too local, or too noisy to standardize.

## Final Principle

A useful framework learns from repetition.

This file exists so repeated business strengths can become shared assets over time.
