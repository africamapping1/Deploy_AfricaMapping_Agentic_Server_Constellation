# Business Summary Discipline

## Purpose

This file defines how summaries inside the business framework should be treated and maintained.

It exists so business summaries remain useful, current, and trustworthy.

This is the summary-discipline layer of the business-framework governance branch.

## Why This Exists

As the business framework grows, summaries become necessary.

But summaries can also become stale, repetitive, or disconnected from the detailed business surfaces they are meant to reflect.

The platform needs discipline here.

## Summary Functions

A business summary should help the platform do one or more of the following:

- understand current state quickly
- compare business surfaces
- review maturity
- support decision-making
- guide next action

## Summary Discipline Rules

### 1. Summary Must Reflect Real Structure
A summary should not invent structure that the detailed business files do not support.

### 2. Summary Must Be Updated After Meaningful Change
When major business definition changes occur, summaries should be reviewed.

### 3. Summary Must Stay Distinct
A summary should not become a copy of every detailed file.

It should remain a compression layer.

### 4. Summary Must Identify Scope
A summary should make clear whether it reflects:

- one business surface
- one tenant
- one framework component
- current state only
- forward-looking interpretation

### 5. Summary Must Avoid False Finality
A summary may be strong while still acknowledging open gaps.

## Important Note

A weak summary reduces trust faster than a missing summary.

If a summary is likely stale or misleading, it should be marked or reviewed.

## Final Principle

A summary is a trust surface.

This file exists so business summaries remain compressed truth rather than drifting noise.
