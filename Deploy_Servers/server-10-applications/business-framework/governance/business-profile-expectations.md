# Business Profile Expectations

## Purpose

This file explains how business expectations may differ by profile inside the business framework.

It exists so the platform does not apply one rigid model to all business surfaces.

This is the profile-expectations layer of the business-framework governance branch.

## Why This Exists

Different business profiles naturally emphasize different strengths.

A role-surface tenant may need different business expression from a stakeholder-service-module tenant.

The framework should preserve comparability without flattening profile differences.

## Core Principle

Profile affects emphasis.

It should not erase shared minimums, but it should influence what strong looks like.

## Example Expectation Patterns

### Role-Surface Style
A role-surface business may be strongest when it clearly defines:

- actors
- responsibilities
- interactions
- workflow position
- service behavior around specific roles

### Stakeholder-Service-Module Style
A stakeholder-service-module business may be strongest when it clearly defines:

- stakeholder groups
- service modules
- business offerings
- module relationships
- external and internal touchpoints

### Hybrid Style
A hybrid business may need to show both:

- role clarity
- module clarity
- stakeholder clarity
- business coherence across multiple surfaces

## Shared Minimums Still Apply

Regardless of profile, the business should still define at least:

- identity
- purpose
- primary value
- primary stakeholders
- core surface
- structural placement

## Important Note

Profile expectation is not a license for vagueness.

It is a way of interpreting strength appropriately.

## Final Principle

Shared minimums support fairness.
Profile-aware expectations support truth.

This file exists so strong businesses can be judged according to the type of business they actually are.
