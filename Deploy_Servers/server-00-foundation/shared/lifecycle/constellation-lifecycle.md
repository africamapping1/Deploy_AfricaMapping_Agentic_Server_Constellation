# Constellation Lifecycle

The Intelligent Server Constellation operates as a governed lifecycle system.

Every new agent, idea, model, policy, workflow, package, or change is routed through:

meet → identify → examine → judge → approve/reject → execute → observe → learn → evolve

## Formal Route

Intake → Identity → Verification → Deliberation → Decision → Execution → Observation → Learning → Evolution

## Meaning of Each Stage

### Meet
Something new arrives into the constellation.
This may be an agent, proposal, package, data source, model, workflow, or operational change.

### Identify
The system determines what the new thing is, where it came from, who owns it, what it claims to do, and what role it should have.

### Examine
The system checks trust, compatibility, relevance, security, policy alignment, and likely impact on the existing constellation.

### Judge
The system weighs the proposed change against existing structure, policy, behavior, and historical decisions.

### Approve / Reject
A governed decision is made.
Some changes are accepted, some rejected, some quarantined, and some returned for refinement.

### Execute
Approved actions are carried out in bounded ways with logs, controls, and rollback awareness.

### Observe
The constellation monitors outcomes, side effects, performance, drift, incidents, and impact.

### Learn
Observed outcomes become memory, rules, scorecards, patterns, and improved future judgment.

### Evolve
The constellation updates itself deliberately through governed change, not uncontrolled drift.

## Operating Principle

No change enters the constellation unexamined.

Nothing new is executed before it is identified, weighed, and judged.

## Re-origination Principle

The constellation is not a system created once and then merely maintained.

It continuously re-originates itself through governed cycles of evaluation, execution, learning, and refinement.

Origin is continuous, not historical.

## Governance Principle

Evolution is not free-form.
It is governed by shared schemas, policies, lifecycle rules, and architectural standards defined in the foundation layer.
